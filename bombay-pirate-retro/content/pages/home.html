The Reading Corner

The Master and Margarita

by Mikhail Bulgakov

A wild, witty, and deeply humane novel about the Devil visiting Soviet Moscow. Part satire, part love story, part meditation on the nature of art and cowardice. One of those books that rearranges the furniture in your head.

Convenience Store Woman

by Sayaka Murata

A short, sharp, oddly serene novel about a woman who finds perfect contentment working at a convenience store while society insists she should want more. Quiet rebellion disguised as compliance.
