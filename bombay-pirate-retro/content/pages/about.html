The Story

Bombay Pirate began as a simple notebook — a place to catalog the interesting threads that kept catching my attention: an obscure open-source project here, a dog-eared paperback there, the way evening light hits the old buildings near Marine Drive.

Over time, the notebook grew into this site. It is not a magazine or a news outlet — it is more of a personal archive, a logbook of curiosities maintained by someone who believes that the best things on the internet are still made by hand, with care, and shared freely.

The name "Bombay Pirate" is a nod to the city's spirit of resourceful invention — and to the idea that good knowledge, like good music, is meant to be shared.

What You'll Find Here

Opensource & WordPress

Notes on the projects, communities, and philosophies that power the open web. From core contributions to plugin discoveries to broader reflections on software freedom.

Books & Movies

Honest, personal notes on what I'm reading and watching. Not reviews — more like letters to a friend about a story that stuck with me on the train ride home.

Photos & Field Notes

Digital photographs from daily life and occasional travels. Street corners, chai stalls, old bookshops, cloud formations. The kind of quiet beauty that disappears if you blink.

If something here resonates, or if you want to talk about a book or a piece of software, feel free to get in touch. The best conversations always start with a shared enthusiasm.
